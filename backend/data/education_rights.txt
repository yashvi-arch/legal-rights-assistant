EDUCATION RIGHTS (India)

1. RIGHT OF CHILDREN TO FREE AND COMPULSORY EDUCATION ACT, 2009 (RTE Act)
Every child aged 6-14 has a fundamental right to free education in a neighborhood school. Private schools must reserve 25% of seats for economically weaker sections and disadvantaged groups, with the government reimbursing the cost.

2. NO DETENTION POLICY (Amended 2019)
Originally, the RTE Act prohibited detention of students until Class 8. This was amended in 2019 to allow states to decide whether to hold back students in Class 5 and Class 8 who fail exams, after providing additional remedial teaching and a re-examination opportunity.

3. PROHIBITION OF CORPORAL PUNISHMENT
Physical punishment and mental harassment of children in schools is banned under the RTE Act, 2009. Teachers and schools can face penalties for violations, and parents can file complaints with the School Management Committee or local education authority.

4. UGC REGULATIONS ON RAGGING
Ragging in colleges and universities is a punishable offense, with penalties ranging from suspension to expulsion and criminal prosecution in serious cases. Anti-Ragging helpline: 1800-180-5522, or report online at antiragging.in.

5. RESERVATION IN HIGHER EDUCATION
Reservations exist for SC (15%), ST (7.5%), OBC (27%), and EWS (10%) categories in central government educational institutions, as mandated by various Constitutional amendments and Supreme Court judgments (subject to the 50% ceiling, with some exceptions).

6. PERSONS WITH DISABILITIES ACT, 2016
Educational institutions must provide reasonable accommodation for students with disabilities, including access to assistive devices, extra time in exams, scribes, and barrier-free infrastructure.

7. UGC GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL
Students facing issues with colleges/universities (fee disputes, denial of admission, harassment) can approach the UGC's online grievance portal or the institution's internal grievance committee.

8. PROTECTION OF CHILDREN FROM SEXUAL OFFENSES ACT, 2012 (POCSO)
Mandates schools to have safety policies and immediate reporting mechanisms for any sexual offense against a child. Teachers and staff are legally required to report suspected abuse — failure to do so is itself a punishable offense.

9. SCHOLARSHIP AND FEE WAIVER RIGHTS
Various central and state government scholarship schemes exist for SC/ST/OBC/EWS students and students with disabilities. The National Scholarship Portal (scholarships.gov.in) consolidates many of these schemes.

How to seek help: For RTE admission issues, contact your district's Block/District Education Officer. For ragging, call 1800-180-5522. For general grievances against educational institutions, use the UGC's grievance portal (ugc.ac.in).
