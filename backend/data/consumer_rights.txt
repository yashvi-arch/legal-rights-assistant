The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 gives Indian consumers six basic rights: right to safety, right to be informed, right to choose, right to be heard, right to seek redressal, and right to consumer education.

How to file a complaint: File online through the e-daakhil portal (edaakhil.nic.in), or call the National Consumer Helpline at 1915 (toll-free) for guidance first.

Where to file: Based on claim value - District Consumer Commission (up to Rs. 1 crore), State Consumer Commission (Rs. 1 crore to Rs. 10 crore), National Consumer Commission (above Rs. 10 crore).

Time limit: File within 2 years from when the issue occurred.

No lawyer required: Consumers can represent themselves; cases move faster than regular civil courts.

Unfair trade practices include: false advertising, defective/substandard goods, overcharging, deficient service, and misleading product claims.
