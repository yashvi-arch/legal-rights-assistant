FAMILY LAW (India)

1. HINDU MARRIAGE ACT, 1955
Governs marriage, divorce, judicial separation, and maintenance for Hindus, Buddhists, Jains, and Sikhs. Grounds for divorce include cruelty, adultery, desertion (2+ years), conversion, mental disorder, and mutual consent (after 1 year of separation).

2. SPECIAL MARRIAGE ACT, 1954
Allows any two Indian citizens, regardless of religion, to marry through a civil ceremony. Commonly used for inter-faith and inter-caste marriages. Requires a 30-day public notice period before the marriage.

3. MUSLIM PERSONAL LAW (Shariat) APPLICATION ACT, 1937
Governs marriage, divorce, inheritance, and maintenance for Muslims based on Islamic personal law, as interpreted by Indian courts. The Triple Talaq (instant divorce) practice was made illegal and a criminal offense under the Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Marriage) Act, 2019.

4. THE INDIAN DIVORCE ACT, 1869 (for Christians)
Governs divorce and judicial separation for Christians in India, with grounds similar to other personal laws (adultery, cruelty, desertion, conversion, etc.).

5. HINDU ADOPTIONS AND MAINTENANCE ACT, 1956
Governs legal adoption procedures and maintenance rights (financial support) for wives, children, and aged parents among Hindus.

6. HINDU SUCCESSION ACT, 1956 (Amended 2005)
Governs inheritance of property for Hindus. The 2005 amendment gave daughters equal rights as sons to inherit ancestral property — a major change from earlier law.

7. PROTECTION OF WOMEN FROM DOMESTIC VIOLENCE ACT, 2005
Provides protection to women facing physical, emotional, sexual, or economic abuse within the household — including from husbands, in-laws, or live-in partners. Remedies include protection orders, residence orders (right to stay in the shared household), and monetary relief. Complaints can be filed with a Protection Officer or directly in court.

8. DOWRY PROHIBITION ACT, 1961
Giving or taking dowry is illegal, punishable with imprisonment (up to 5 years) and fines. Section 304B of the IPC (now BNS) also covers "dowry death" if a woman dies under suspicious circumstances within 7 years of marriage due to dowry harassment.

9. GUARDIANS AND WARDS ACT, 1890
Governs custody and guardianship of minor children when parents separate or in cases where a guardian must be appointed. Courts decide custody based on the "best interest of the child."

10. MAINTENANCE UNDER SECTION 125 CrPC (now Section 144 BNSS)
Any wife, child, or parent who is unable to maintain themselves can claim monthly maintenance from a husband/son with sufficient means, regardless of religion. This is a fast, simple legal remedy compared to personal law proceedings.

11. THE JUVENILE JUSTICE (CARE AND PROTECTION OF CHILDREN) ACT, 2015
Governs adoption procedures (via CARA - Central Adoption Resource Authority), juvenile offenders, and children in need of care and protection.

How to seek help: Contact a Family Court for divorce/custody matters, the nearest Protection Officer or police Women's Cell for domestic violence, or the National Commission for Women helpline (7827170170) for guidance.
