Tenant rights in India mainly come from state-specific Rent Control Acts, though the central government introduced the Model Tenancy Act, 2021 as a template (state laws vary, so always check your specific state).

Security deposit: Model Tenancy Act suggests max 2 months' rent for residential, 6 months for non-residential - but actual state limits differ.

Written agreement: A written, registered rental agreement protects both tenant and landlord and is needed as proof in any dispute.

Eviction: A landlord cannot evict by force or arbitrarily. It requires proper written notice and, if disputed, must go through a Rent Court/Authority - not by cutting off water or electricity.

Rent increase: Should follow what's agreed in the rental contract; arbitrary mid-term hikes aren't allowed.

Repairs: Structural repairs are usually the landlord's job; minor maintenance is usually the tenant's, unless agreed otherwise.

Cutting off essential supplies (water/electricity) during a dispute is illegal in most states.
