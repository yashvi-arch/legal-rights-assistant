Reporting cybercrime in India: File at the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in), or call the Cyber Crime Helpline at 1930. The portal has a special category for crimes against women and children and allows anonymous complaints in sensitive cases.

Other helplines: National Women Helpline - 181. Police emergency - 112.

Legal framework: Since July 1, 2024, the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023 replaced the IPC and covers offences against women including sexual harassment, stalking, voyeurism, and assault to outrage modesty. The IT Act, 2000 separately covers online offences - identity theft, cheating by impersonation, privacy violations (sharing private images without consent), and publishing obscene material online.

Common cyber offences against women: online harassment, stalking, circulating morphed/obscene images, blackmail using private photos, fake profiles, financial fraud via impersonation.

What to do: Save evidence (screenshots, links, messages) before reporting, don't delete anything, report quickly. For serious/urgent threats, also call police (112) directly.

Note: This is general information, not legal advice.
