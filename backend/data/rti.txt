The Right to Information Act, 2005 lets any Indian citizen request information from government departments and public authorities.

How to file an RTI: Write a request mentioning the specific information you want, address it to the Public Information Officer (PIO) of the relevant department. Submit it online at rtionline.gov.in (for central government departments) or by post with the fee.

Fee: Usually Rs. 10 for central government departments (can vary by state, and is waived for applicants below the poverty line).

Response time: The department must respond within 30 days. If the request involves a matter affecting someone's life or liberty, they must respond within 48 hours.

If denied or no response: You can file a first appeal with the appellate authority in the same department. If still unsatisfied, file a second appeal with the Central or State Information Commission.

What's exempted: National security matters, cabinet papers, and personal information with no public interest connection cannot be requested.

Note: RTI applies to government bodies and organizations substantially funded by the government — not private companies.
